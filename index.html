Traditional Farming Practices in Africa

Small-scale farmers across Africa have developed sustainable and effective farming practices over generations. These methods are often well-adapted to local conditions and resources, representing a rich agricultural heritage.

Crop Rotation

Planting different crops in succession to maintain soil fertility and reduce pest problems. This ancient practice helps prevent soil depletion and breaks pest cycles naturally.

Water Conservation

Traditional irrigation methods like terracing, check dams, and water harvesting techniques help small-scale farmers make the most of limited water resources.

Natural Pest Control

Using companion planting, natural predators, and plant-based repellents to manage pests without synthetic chemicals, preserving ecosystem balance.

When should I plant tomatoes in Zone 7?

In Zone 7, the best time to plant tomatoes is after the last frost date, typically mid-April to early May. Soil temperature should be at least 60°F (15.5°C).
